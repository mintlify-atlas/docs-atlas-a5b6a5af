## Discovering Opportunities

### Browsing the Feed

The opportunities feed displays a curated list of active opportunities. Each card shows:

- **Opportunity Title** and **Organizer Information**
- **Opportunity Type** (Hackathon, Grant, Competition, or Ideathon)
- **Location** and **Date Range** (start and end dates)
- **Images** showcasing the event
- **Tags** for quick categorization (AI, Biology, MBA, Startup, Psychology, Web3)
- **Upvote Count** indicating community interest

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop View">
    The desktop layout features a 3-column design:
    - **Left Sidebar**: Search bar, quick links, and featured opportunities
    - **Main Feed**: Scrollable opportunity cards with infinite loading
    - **Right Sidebar**: Calendar widget showing upcoming deadlines and task widget
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile View">
    Mobile users get a streamlined single-column feed with:
    - Top search bar with filter button
    - Horizontal tag badges for quick filtering
    - Optimized card layout for smaller screens
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Search and Filters

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search by Keywords">
    Use the search bar to find opportunities by title, organizer, or description. The search updates automatically as you type (400ms debounce).
    
    **Example searches**: "DU Hacks", "Tech meetup", "Fellowship"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Type">
    Click the filter icon and select from:
    - Hackathon
    - Grant
    - Competition
    - Ideathon
    
    You can select multiple types simultaneously.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by Tags">
    Click on tag badges to filter by domain:
    - AI
    - Biology
    - MBA
    - Startup
    - Psychology
    - Web3
    
    Selected tags are highlighted in dark gray.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clear Filters">
    Click "Clear Filters" to reset all search criteria and view the full feed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All filter changes are reflected in the URL, so you can bookmark or share specific filtered views.
</Note>

## Interacting with Opportunities

### Viewing Details

Click on any opportunity card to view:
- Full description and requirements
- Complete image gallery with modal view
- Organizer contact information
- Start and end dates
- Comments from other users

### Bookmarking

Save opportunities to your personal tracker:

1. Click the **Bookmark** icon on any opportunity card
2. The opportunity is automatically added to your Tracker with "Not Applied" status
3. Access your saved opportunities from the Tracker page

<Warning>
  You must be logged in to bookmark opportunities. Guest users will see a login prompt.
</Warning>

### Upvoting

Show support for quality opportunities by upvoting:

- Click the upvote arrow to increase the count
- Upvoted opportunities appear with a highlighted upvote button
- High upvote counts indicate community-verified quality

### Commenting

Engage with the community:

1. Click the comment icon on an opportunity
2. Write your question, experience, or tip
3. View replies from organizers and other students

## Posting Opportunities

### Creating a New Opportunity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click 'Post Opportunity'">
    Find the button in the horizontal banner (desktop) or vertical card (mobile) at the top of the feed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in Details">
    Required fields:
    - **Title**: Clear, descriptive name
    - **Type**: Select from Hackathon, Grant, Competition, or Ideathon
    - **Description**: Detailed information about the opportunity
    - **Organizer Info**: Contact details or organization name
    
    Optional fields:
    - **Images**: Upload event posters or promotional material
    - **Location**: Physical or virtual venue
    - **Start Date** and **End Date**
    - **Tags**: Categorize your opportunity (up to 6 tags)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit for Review">
    New opportunities are published immediately but may be flagged for admin review if they violate community guidelines.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Only verified users can post opportunities. Unverified accounts can browse and bookmark.
</Info>

### Editing Your Posts

If you created an opportunity:

1. Open the opportunity detail view
2. Click the **Edit** button (visible only to the creator)
3. Update any field and save changes
4. Your edits are reflected immediately

## Featured Opportunities

The left sidebar (desktop) shows **Featured Opportunities** - a curated selection of high-quality, trending opportunities. These are typically:

- Recently posted opportunities with high engagement
- Verified opportunities from trusted organizations
- Time-sensitive opportunities with approaching deadlines

## Calendar Integration

The right sidebar displays a **Calendar Widget** showing:

- Colored dots on dates with upcoming opportunity deadlines
- Quick view of this month's important dates
- Click any date to filter opportunities by deadline

<Tip>
  Bookmark opportunities to see their deadlines highlighted in the calendar widget.
</Tip>

## Best Practices

  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I know if an opportunity is legitimate?">
    Look for:
    - Verification badges on the post
    - High upvote counts from the community
    - Complete organizer information with official contact details
    - Professional images and detailed descriptions
    
    You can also comment to ask questions or search for the organization online.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I share opportunities with friends?">
    Yes! Each opportunity has a unique URL. Simply copy the link from your browser's address bar when viewing the opportunity detail page.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What happens to expired opportunities?">
    Opportunities with end dates in the past may be filtered out or marked as expired. However, they remain visible for reference and comments from participants.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How are opportunities ranked in the feed?">
    The default feed shows:
    1. Pinned/featured opportunities first
    2. Recently posted opportunities
    3. Opportunities sorted by engagement (upvotes + comments)
    
    You can use filters to customize the order.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
